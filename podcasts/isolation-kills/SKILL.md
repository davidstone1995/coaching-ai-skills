# Isolation Kills Podcast

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## The Show

**Podcast:** Isolation Kills
**Entity:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions
**Core conviction:** "Isolation kills. Connection heals."

The premise is simple and serious: the most dangerous thing a person can do is disappear into themselves. This podcast exists to name that danger and point people toward connection -- with others, with themselves, and with God when that door is open.

David is not a detached host. He is a coach, a chaplain, and a man who has sat with people in isolation for thirty years. Every episode is grounded in that experience.

## Episode Planning

### Suggest a Topic

If David doesn't have a topic, suggest one from this pool of categories. Each category has produced strong episodes and has more to give.

**Categories:**
- The anatomy of isolation (how it starts, what it looks like, what it costs)
- Connection killers (pride, shame, busyness, unresolved conflict, perfectionism)
- Connection builders (vulnerability, presence, confession, ritual, community)
- Loneliness in specific populations (athletes, pastors, entrepreneurs, men, grieving people)
- The role of faith in connection (community, spiritual isolation, the church's responsibility)
- Personal stories (David's own experience, guest stories, turning-point moments)
- Practical tools (how to reach out, how to receive care, how to build rhythms of connection)

### Episode Outline

Produce a complete episode outline with intro, main sections, and close. Calibrate to format.

---

**EPISODE OUTLINE**
Episode [#]: [Title]
Format: [Solo / Guest Interview / Co-host]
Target length: [20-30 min solo / 35-50 min interview]

---

**COLD OPEN (30-60 seconds)**

A single moment, image, or question that drops the listener into the episode before David introduces himself or the show. Not a summary of what's coming. A moment that makes them want to stay.

Examples:
- A specific scene from a chaplaincy visit or coaching session (anonymized)
- A question the listener is probably already asking
- A stark statistic held without comment
- A line that names something true that most people feel but don't say

---

**INTRO (1-2 minutes)**

Brief. David introduces the episode and why this topic matters right now -- not in general, but this week, this moment. What is happening in the world or in the lives of people listening that makes this the right conversation for today?

---

**MAIN CONTENT (15-35 minutes)**

*For solo episodes:* 3-4 sections. Each section makes one point. No more. Structure:
- Section 1: Name the problem or reality
- Section 2: Why it happens / what drives it
- Section 3: What connection actually looks like in this area
- Section 4 (optional): What David has learned from coaching or chaplaincy on this topic

*For interview episodes:* 5-7 questions, sequenced from accessible to deep. See Guest Prep section for question development. The outline notes the arc: where the guest starts, what the conversation should surface, where David wants to land by the end.

---

**CLOSE (2-3 minutes)**

Three elements, in order:

1. One takeaway: The single most important thing from the episode. Said simply. David's voice, not a summary.
2. The invitation: Something the listener can do before the next episode. Specific. Actionable. Connected to the topic.
3. The sign-off: Brief, warm, consistent. "Isolation kills. Connection heals. I'll see you next time." Or a variation that fits the episode's tone.

## Guest Prep

Produce a complete guest prep package: a one-page brief for David to read before the interview, and a question set for the episode.

---

**GUEST BRIEF**
[Guest Name] | Episode [#] | [Date]

**Who they are (in David's voice):**
[2-3 sentences. Not their bio -- who they are as a person and what they bring to this conversation. What David knows about them or needs to know before the interview.]

**Why them for this episode:**
[One sentence. What makes this person the right voice for this specific topic at this specific time.]

**What to listen for:**
[2-3 things David should pay attention to during the interview. What this person tends to say that is worth slowing down for. What they might avoid that is worth gently surfacing.]

**One question to hold:**
[The question David carries into the interview that he may or may not ask depending on where the conversation goes. The question underneath the questions.]

---

**INTERVIEW QUESTION SET**

7-9 questions. In sequence. Each question earns the next.

Structure:
- Q1-2: Entry questions. Accessible. Let the guest settle in and establish their credibility with the audience.
- Q3-4: The story. Something personal, specific, and real. The moment that shaped their perspective on this topic.
- Q5-6: The depth. Where the conversation goes somewhere most interviewers don't take it. The honest, uncomfortable, important territory.
- Q7-8: The practical. What the listener can do with everything they just heard.
- Q9: The close. One final question that gives the guest the last word and leaves the listener with something to sit with.

**Transition notes:** Note where David might want to pause, affirm, or follow a thread before moving to the next question. Interview prep is not a script. It is a compass.
