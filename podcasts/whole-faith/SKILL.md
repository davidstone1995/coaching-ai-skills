# Whole:Faith Church and Mental Health Podcast

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## The Show

**Podcast:** Whole:Faith
**Full title:** Whole:Faith Church and Mental Health
**Entity:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions
**The premise:** The church has not always done mental health well. Whole:Faith exists to change that -- one honest conversation at a time. For pastors who don't know what to say. For church leaders who want to do better. For people of faith navigating mental health who feel like they have to choose between their beliefs and their wellbeing.

David's position is not neutral. He is a pastor who has sat with hurting people for thirty years, a coach with mental health training, and a man who takes both faith and mental health seriously. He is not asking whether faith and mental health can coexist. He is showing how.

## Episode Planning

### Suggest a Topic

Draw from these categories when David doesn't have a topic ready. Each represents a recurring need in the church-and-mental-health conversation.

**For pastors and church leaders:**
- What to say (and not say) when someone shares a mental health struggle
- Building a mental health culture in your church without a full counseling team
- Pastoral burnout: when the shepherd needs a shepherd
- Preaching on mental health without getting it wrong
- The difference between spiritual direction and counseling -- and why churches blur it
- How to refer well (what to say, who to call, when to step back)

**For congregants and people of faith:**
- "Why can't I just pray this away?" -- faith, mental health, and the limits of both
- Anxiety, depression, and doubt in the same season
- What the church gets right about mental health -- and what it gets wrong
- Finding community when you're struggling and church feels like performance
- Medication, therapy, and faith: navigating all three without shame

**For the intersection:**
- The mental health crisis in ministry (pastors, missionaries, church planters)
- Trauma-informed church culture
- Children and youth mental health -- what churches can do
- Grief, loss, and the church's role
- Suicide loss and the church -- how to respond, how to support
- Mental health and spiritual warfare: where the line is and how to talk about it carefully

**COLD OPEN (30-60 seconds)**

A real moment. A specific scenario. A question that people have been carrying alone. No show intro yet -- just the thing that makes them need to stay for the rest of the episode.

Examples:
- A pastor describing the moment they realized they had no idea how to respond to someone in crisis
- A question a church member is afraid to ask out loud
- A statistic that reframes the scale of the problem
- A brief story (anonymized) from David's own experience with a pastor or congregant

The tone of the cold open sets the tone of the episode. Whole:Faith episodes are honest, not alarmist. Caring, not clinical.

---

**INTRO (1-2 minutes)**

David introduces the episode. Why this topic, why now, why him. Not a bio -- a reason. What is happening in churches, in families, in the culture that makes this the right conversation for this episode?

---

**MAIN CONTENT (20-45 minutes)**

*For solo episodes:* 3-4 sections. One point per section.
- Section 1: Name the reality honestly. What is actually happening in churches around this topic?
- Section 2: Where the church has fallen short -- said with grace, not blame
- Section 3: What faithfulness looks like. Practical, specific, doable
- Section 4 (optional): What David has seen work -- from pastoral experience and coaching

*For interview episodes:* 6-8 questions sequenced from context to depth to practical. See Guest Prep section. Note the arc: what the guest brings to the first question, what needs to surface by the middle, and where the conversation should land for the listener.

**A note on tone throughout:** Whole:Faith episodes hold tension well. Faith is not dismissed. Mental health is not pathologized. Both are taken seriously. If an episode tips too far toward either -- too clinical, or too spiritualized -- it has lost its audience. Every episode should be something a thoughtful pastor and a thoughtful therapist would both respect.

---

**CLOSE (2-3 minutes)**

1. One takeaway: The single most important thing for a listener to carry out. Phrased simply. Actionable if possible.
2. The invitation: One specific thing a listener can do this week. For pastors: something they can do in their ministry. For congregants: something they can do for themselves or someone they care about.
3. The sign-off: Warm, consistent, brief. Something that reminds listeners what this show is about and why they came back.

---

**INTERVIEW QUESTION SET**

7-9 questions. Sequenced from accessible to honest to practical.

Structure:
- Q1-2: Entry. Guest establishes who they are and why this topic is personal to them. The listener needs to trust the guest before the hard questions.
- Q3-4: The story or the work. A specific experience that shaped their understanding of faith and mental health together. This is where Whole:Faith guests usually go somewhere other shows don't reach.
- Q5-6: The honest territory. What the church gets wrong. What it gets right. What the guest wishes pastors or congregants understood. This is the section that makes people share the episode.
- Q7-8: The practical. What a listener can do -- whether they're a pastor, a church member, or a mental health professional. Specific. Actionable.
- Q9: The close. A final question that gives the guest the last word and leaves the listener with something to carry.

**Note on faith language in interviews:** With guests who are explicitly people of faith, faith language is natural and welcome. With mental health professionals who may not share David's faith background, let them lead. David's job is not to evangelize in the interview -- it is to have the most honest conversation possible about the intersection of faith and mental health. Trust the topic to carry the weight.
