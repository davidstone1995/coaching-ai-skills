# Session Prep

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

The best coaching sessions look effortless. They are not. Preparation is what makes them feel that way.

A pre-session brief serves one purpose: help David show up empty of distraction and full of presence. Not with a script. Not with an agenda to complete. With a quiet mind, a clear picture of who this person is, and a handful of questions held lightly -- ready to be set aside the moment the client takes the conversation somewhere more important.

This skill generates two things: a pre-session brief David reads before the call, and a post-session note template he fills in after.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Client name
- Client type (pastor/ministry leader, church staff, business owner/entrepreneur, communication coaching, premarital couple, Team Care/athlete)
- Session number (first session, ongoing, gifted session)

**Helpful:**
- What the client has been working on (last session's focus or action steps)
- Anything the client mentioned since the last session (email, message, etc.)
- Any context David is holding about this person's situation
- Specific topic or area the client may bring (if known)

If this is a first session, a gifted session, or if no prior context is available, the skill generates an open prep brief.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Session Context

**First session / gifted session:** No prior coaching history. The brief focuses on how to open well, how to establish presence quickly, and how to earn the right to ask deeper questions. Goal: get them talking about something real within the first five minutes.

**Ongoing session:** Prior context exists. The brief connects the thread from the last session, notes what the client committed to, and holds questions that may surface if the work went well -- or if it didn't.

**Premarital session:** Different arc. SYMBIS and Prepare and Enrich content governs the structure. Brief focuses on what topic is on the agenda for this session and the couple's dynamic from prior sessions.

**Team Care / chaplaincy visit:** Not a coaching session. See visit-prep skill instead.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Pre-Session Brief

One page. David reads this in the 10 minutes before the session. It is not a script -- it is a frame.

---

**PRE-SESSION BRIEF**
[Client Name] | Session [#] | [Date] | [Entity: Cornerstone Coaching Solutions]

---

**Who is coming into the room**

[2-3 sentences. Remind David who this person is -- not their bio, but their situation. What they are carrying. What they want. What gets in their way. Write it like David is reminding himself of someone he cares about, not summarizing a file.]

**Where we left off**

[What happened in the last session, or what David knows going in. The focus they named. The action step they took on. If this is a first session: "This is our first time. Arrive without assumptions. Let them set the table."]

**What to hold**

[2-4 questions David carries into the room. Not questions to ask -- questions to hold. Questions that may become relevant depending on where the session goes. Drawn from what the client is working on, what patterns have surfaced, or what might be lying just below the surface.]

Examples of the kind of questions to generate:
- "What are they avoiding naming?"
- "What would it cost them to actually change this?"
- "What are they getting from staying stuck?"
- "Who else is affected by this that they haven't mentioned?"
- "What would future-them say to current-them right now?"

**What to notice**

[1-2 patterns or dynamics worth paying attention to. Not predictions -- awareness. Example: "They tend to move quickly to solutions before sitting with the problem. If that happens today, slow it down." Or: "They haven't talked about [X person] in two sessions. Notice if that comes up."]

**The Coaching Sandwich arc for today**

*Top Bread:* "What would you like to focus on today?" / "What would success look like for you by the end of our time?"

*Lettuce:* Reflect back. Explore impact. Notice energy. "What impact is this having on you right now?" / "What's at stake for you here?"

*Tomato:* Confirm before moving. "After everything you've shared, what needs to happen today to feel complete?"

*Protein:* [Suggested awareness questions calibrated to what you know about this client and session. 3-4 options to hold lightly, not follow in sequence.]

*Cheese/Veggies:* "What action step can you take?" / "What is the first step?" / "What can you do on this call right now?"

*Bottom Bread:* "What did you get from this call?" / "What have you learned about yourself?" / "What was your biggest insight?" / "What would you tell yourself based on today?"

**One thing to remember**

[One sentence. The thing that matters most for this session. Not advice -- a reminder. Example: "Their first answer is rarely their real answer. Sit with the silence." Or: "They are harder on themselves than the situation warrants. Don't let them off the hook, but don't pile on either."]

## Step 3: Generate the Post-Session Note Template

Fill this in within 30 minutes of the session while it's still fresh.

---

**POST-SESSION NOTE**
[Client Name] | Session [#] | [Date]

**Focus:** [What the client actually wanted to work on -- not what David expected]

**Key moment:** [The turn in the session. The place where something shifted -- a question that landed, a moment of silence, a realization. One to two sentences.]

**What they said that mattered:** [A direct quote or near-quote. The thing the client said that they need to hear again.]

**Action step they named:** [Exactly what they committed to. Their words, not a paraphrase. This gets referenced at the start of next session.]

**What I noticed:** [Pattern, dynamic, or energy worth tracking. Not for the client -- for David's awareness going into the next session.]

**For next session:** [One or two things to carry forward. What to open with. What question to hold.]

**Referral or follow-up needed?** [Yes / No. If yes: what and by when.]

---

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the brief:

- Pre-session brief is one page or less
- Questions held are specific to this client, not generic coaching prompts
- Protein section has 3-4 calibrated questions -- not a transcript of the Coaching Sandwich
- "One thing to remember" is a real insight, not a platitude
- Post-session template has blank fields ready to fill in
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
- Entity is correct (Cornerstone unless flagged as Team Care)
