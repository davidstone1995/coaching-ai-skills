# Assessment Debrief

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

Assessments are tools. The debrief is where the work happens. A client can read their own results -- what they cannot do is sit with someone who knows them, holds the results without judgment, and asks the questions that help them make meaning of what they're seeing.

This skill prepares David to run that conversation. It produces a debrief prep guide with key talking points, a conversation arc, and questions calibrated to what the results may reveal. It does not reproduce proprietary content verbatim.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Assessment type: LPI, SYMBIS, or Prepare and Enrich
- Client name and type
- Any results or scores you have (provide whatever you have -- even partial results are enough to work with)

**Helpful:**
- What you already know about this person that the results might confirm or complicate
- Any specific area the client flagged as important to them before seeing results
- Whether this is a first debrief or a follow-up to a prior assessment

## Step 1: Identify the Assessment Context

### LPI (Leadership Practices Inventory)

A 360-degree leadership assessment measuring Five Practices: Model the Way, Inspire a Shared Vision, Challenge the Process, Enable Others to Act, and Encourage the Heart. The debrief compares the leader's self-rating against rater (observer) ratings across each practice.

The most important moment in an LPI debrief is often not the lowest score -- it is the gap between self-perception and how others see them. A high self-rating with low observer ratings is a blind spot. A low self-rating with high observer ratings is a strength the leader does not own. Both need time.

**LPI debrief context:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions. Usually with business owners, church staff, or ministry leaders. Can be individual or team.

### SYMBIS (Save Your Marriage Before It Starts)

A premarital assessment tool (licensed through Les and Leslie Parrott). Produces a personalized couple report covering emotional health, communication, conflict resolution, expectations, personalities, and spirituality.

**SYMBIS debrief context:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions, premarital coaching. David is licensed to deliver. Do not reproduce SYMBIS-specific content or scoring. Focus on the conversation arc and questions that open the couple to honest dialogue.

### Prepare and Enrich

A premarital and marriage enrichment assessment (licensed). Similar scope to SYMBIS -- covers communication, conflict resolution, family of origin, finances, expectations, and spiritual beliefs. Results include a couple's strengths and growth areas.

**Prepare and Enrich debrief context:** Same as SYMBIS. David is licensed to deliver. Same content restrictions apply.

## Step 2: Generate the Debrief Prep Guide

---

### LPI DEBRIEF GUIDE

**[Client Name] | LPI Debrief | [Date] | Cornerstone Coaching Solutions**

---

**Before the conversation**

The goal of the debrief is not to explain the assessment. They can read the report. The goal is to help them make meaning of what they are seeing -- and to use what they find to take one real step forward.

Resist the pull to teach or interpret. Ask. Reflect. Let them arrive at their own insight. That is the only kind that sticks.

---

**Opening the debrief (Top Bread)**

> "Before we get into the results, I want to ask: what did you notice as you filled out the self-assessment? Anything surprising -- or anything you've been thinking about since you completed it?"

Let them lead. The report can wait. What they are already thinking about is more important.

> "As we go through this, I want to focus on what matters most to you -- not just what the numbers say. So at any point, tell me where you want to slow down."

---

**The Five Practices -- conversation arc**

For each practice with a notable score (high, low, or gap):

1. **Name it without interpretation:** "Let's look at [Practice Name]. Your score was [X] and your raters averaged [Y]. What's your reaction to that?"

2. **Explore the gap if one exists:**
   - High self / low rater: "If your raters saw [practice] differently than you did -- what do you think they're seeing?"
   - Low self / high rater: "Your raters scored you higher here than you scored yourself. What do you make of that?"

3. **Awareness questions (Protein layer):**
   - "Where do you feel most confident in how you [practice]?"
   - "Where does this feel hardest for you?"
   - "What would it look like if you did this 10% better?"
   - "Who in your world models this practice well? What do they do that you don't?"

4. **Move toward action:**
   - "If you were going to take one step in this area before our next session, what would it be?"
   - "What would your team need to see from you to believe you were growing in this?"

---

**Key questions to hold for this client**

[Generate 3-4 questions based on what David has shared about the client and their results. Calibrate to what this specific person's blind spots or growth areas likely are. Example prompts:
- "What does their lowest-rated practice say about the pressure they are under?"
- "What does the gap between self and rater tell us about how they receive feedback?"
- "What would owning their highest-rated practice more consciously do for them?"]

---

**Closing the debrief (Bottom Bread)**

> "Before we close, I want to ask: what are you taking away from today? What are you going to do with it?"

Confirm the action step. Write it in their words, not yours.

### SYMBIS / PREPARE AND ENRICH DEBRIEF GUIDE

**[Couple Names] | [SYMBIS / Prepare and Enrich] Debrief | [Date] | Cornerstone Coaching Solutions**

---

**Before the conversation**

Premarital debrief is not about identifying problems. It is about building the kind of honest communication now that makes the hard conversations easier later. Most couples come in wanting to be told they are fine. David's job is to be glad they are fine AND to help them find the one or two things worth talking about before they are living together.

Hold both people equally. If one partner dominates the conversation, redirect with care.

---

**Opening the debrief (Top Bread)**

> "Before we look at the results together, I want to start with you two. In the time since you completed the assessment, has anything come up -- any conversations, any questions, anything you've been thinking about?"

Let them set the table. Then:

> "The assessment gives us a framework, but the conversation is the real work. If we do this well today, you'll leave with at least one thing to talk about before the next session -- something you've been meaning to say to each other but haven't quite found the words for yet."

---

**Debrief conversation arc**

Work through the couple's results area by area (communication, conflict, expectations, family of origin, finances, spiritual beliefs -- calibrated to which tool is being used).

For each area:

1. **Name what the results show:** "In the area of [topic], the assessment flagged [strength / growth area]. What does that feel like as you hear it?"

2. **Give each partner space:** Ask both. Don't let one answer for the other.

3. **Awareness questions:**
   - "What does [partner's name] do in [this area] that you appreciate most?"
   - "What has been the hardest part of [this area] in your relationship so far?"
   - "What did your family of origin model here -- and is that what you want to carry forward?"
   - "What do you need from [partner] in [this area] that you haven't fully said yet?"

4. **Move toward one honest moment:** The goal of each section is one real exchange between the two of them -- something true, said kindly, that they needed to say before the wedding.

---

**Topics to slow down on**

[Based on results provided, flag 1-2 areas where the couple's responses suggest more to explore. Example: "Their conflict resolution scores diverge significantly -- she rated it higher. Slow down there and ask what each of them does when they are most upset."]

---

**Closing**

> "What are you taking away from today? What is one thing you want to keep talking about before our next session?"

Confirm the conversation they are going to have. Name it specifically.

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the guide:

- Debrief arc follows the Coaching Sandwich structure (establish focus, explore, confirm, awareness, action, close)
- Assessment-specific questions are calibrated to the client and results provided
- No proprietary content reproduced verbatim (SYMBIS, Prepare and Enrich)
- LPI debrief distinguishes between self/rater gap and absolute scores
- Premarital debrief holds both partners equally
- One clear action or takeaway is named in the closing of each arc
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
