# New Client Intake

**Requires:** coaching-foundation skill

---

## What This Skill Does

Most new clients come in through a gifted session -- a complimentary first coaching hour offered through the Insight Coaching Community. The intake package serves two purposes: it prepares David to make that first session genuinely valuable, and it positions ongoing coaching as the natural next step without a hard sell.

A great intake communicates three things before the first session starts: David takes this seriously, the client's time matters, and something real is going to happen in that room.

The gifted session is not a preview. It is the real thing. David shows up fully present, asks real questions, and trusts the process. The intake package sets the tone so the client arrives ready to work.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Client name
- Client type (pastor/ministry leader, church staff, business owner/entrepreneur, communication coaching, premarital couple, or other)
- How they came in (gifted session, direct referral, other)

**Helpful:**
- What you already know about them
- Any specific area they've mentioned wanting to work on
- Whether this is Cornerstone or Team Care context

If you only have a name and client type, that is enough to generate the full package.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Client Context

Before generating anything, determine:

1. **Client type.** This shapes the language, the questions, and the tone throughout. A pastor and a business owner need different entry points even if the coaching process is the same.

2. **Entry point.** Gifted session clients need slightly different framing than clients who sought David out directly. Gifted session framing: "You've been given a gift. Let's make the most of it." Direct referral framing: "Someone you trust pointed you here. Here's what to expect."

3. **Cornerstone or Team Care.** For chaplaincy contexts (corporate client, MSU athletics), the intake is lighter and more relational. For coaching clients, it is structured and professional. Default to Cornerstone unless David specifies Team Care.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Intake Package

Produce all four components below. Each is a complete, ready-to-use deliverable.

---

### COMPONENT 1: Intake Questionnaire

A short form sent before the first session. The goal is not to gather data for a file -- it is to get the client thinking about themselves before they walk in the door. Seven to ten questions maximum.

Structure:

**Opening line (not a question):**
One sentence telling the client why this matters. Example: "Your answers help me show up prepared so we spend your time on what matters most to you."

**Section 1: Who You Are (2-3 questions)**
- [A question about their current role, what they do, and how long they've been doing it]
- [A question about what they're most proud of in that role right now]
- [Optional: a question about their biggest responsibility or pressure point at the moment]

**Section 2: Why You're Here (2-3 questions)**
- [What they're hoping to get from coaching -- keep it open-ended, not leading]
- [What has already gotten in the way of making progress on this on their own]
- [What would make them consider this session a success]

**Section 3: Quick Context (2-3 questions)**
- [Whether they've worked with a coach before, and if so, what was useful or not]
- [How they prefer to receive feedback -- direct, gentle, a mix]
- [Anything they want David to know before they meet]

**Closing line:**
One warm sentence. Not a legal disclaimer. Example: "See you soon. Come ready to think out loud."

**Calibration by client type:**
- **Pastors/ministry leaders:** Include a question about who pours into them. These clients rarely get asked that.
- **Business owners:** Include a question about where the business is taking their energy vs. where they want it to go.
- **Premarital couples:** Separate forms for each partner. Add a question about what they most want the other person to understand about them.
- **Communication coaching:** Include a question about a recent high-stakes communication moment and how it went.
- **Team Care / chaplaincy:** Keep it to three questions max. Informal. No form structure.

---

### COMPONENT 2: Welcome Email

Sent immediately after the first contact is confirmed. Warm, brief, professional. This email sets the tone for the entire engagement.

Structure:

**Subject line:** "You're All Set -- Here's What to Expect" (default for gifted session confirmation). Adjust for ongoing coaching clients.

**Default gifted session confirmation email (use this as the base, personalize before sending):**

> Subject: You're All Set -- Here's What to Expect
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I am looking forward to our conversation on [Day], [Date] at [Time].
>
> Zoom link: [link]
>
> Before we meet: Show up on time -- early if possible. Find a quiet, distraction-free space. Close the door. Put the phone down. Bring a notebook and pen. Come with something in mind -- a goal, a challenge, something you want to work through.
>
> Treat this session like you invested everything you have into it. Because in a real sense, you did.
>
> I will be there a few minutes early. See you soon. -- David

**For clients who filled out the intake form:** Acknowledge one specific thing they shared. One sentence. It signals that David actually read it.

**What to avoid:**
- Paragraphs longer than three sentences
- Any phrase that sounds like a marketing email
- Attaching anything other than the intake form at this stage
- Asking them to do anything beyond filling out the form and showing up

---

### COMPONENT 3: What to Expect Document

A one-page document the client reads before or after the first session. Answers the questions clients have but don't always ask. Plain language. No jargon.

Structure:

**Header:** "What to Expect from Coaching with David Stone | Cornerstone Coaching Solutions"

**Section 1: What coaching is (3-4 sentences)**
Coaching is not consulting, therapy, or mentoring. David is not going to tell the client what to do. He is going to ask the questions that help the client hear what they already know. The work happens in the conversation.

**Section 2: What a session looks like**
- Sessions are one hour.
- The client sets the agenda. David asks where they want to start.
- The session ends with at least one clear next step the client owns.
- Sessions are confidential. What is said in the session stays there.

**Section 3: What the client's job is**
- Show up. Be on time.
- Come with something real to work on. This is not a check-in call.
- Be honest. The quality of the coaching is directly tied to the quality of the conversation.
- Do what you said you would do between sessions.

**Section 4: Practical details**
- Sessions are one hour, typically bi-weekly or monthly
- Format: in-person in Springfield, MO or Zoom
- Scheduling: through Google Calendar (David will send a link)
- Between-session contact: not available. Sessions are where the work happens. This boundary protects the quality of the coaching.
- Office hours: Monday through Friday, 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
- Cancellation: 24 hours notice required. Late cancellations or no-shows forfeit the session.

**Footer:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions | cornerstonecoaching.solutions

**Calibration note:** For gifted session clients, add a brief paragraph: "This session is a gift. There is no expectation of a commitment beyond today. If ongoing coaching feels like a fit at the end of our time, we can talk about what that looks like. If not, the session is still yours."

---

### COMPONENT 4: Coaching Agreement

A simple, plain-language agreement. Not a legal contract -- a mutual commitment that sets expectations and protects both parties. One to two pages maximum.

Structure:

**Header:** Cornerstone Coaching Solutions Coaching Agreement

**Parties:**
This agreement is between David Stone, Cornerstone Coaching Solutions LLC, and [Client Name].

**What coaching is and is not:**
Two to three sentences clarifying that coaching is a professional relationship focused on the client's goals, distinct from therapy, counseling, or consulting. David is not a licensed therapist. Clients with clinical mental health needs will be referred to appropriate professionals.

**Commitment:**
- Sessions are [frequency] and [duration] minutes each.
- Client agrees to arrive prepared and on time.
- Either party may end the engagement with [X days] notice.

**Confidentiality:**
Everything discussed in sessions is confidential. David will not share client information with any third party without written consent, except where required by law.

**Fees and scheduling:**
- Gifted session: Mark clearly -- "This engagement begins with a complimentary gifted session. There is no obligation beyond today."
- 1:1 Coaching (3 months): $1,500/month or $4,000 pay-in-full (saves $500)
- Per session rate: $175/session
- Premarital (with officiant): $850 / 5 sessions. Coaching only: $600 / 4 sessions.
- Payment via Stripe invoice (sent through GoHighLevel / CV Pro Tools)
- Cancellation: 24 hours notice required. Late cancellation or no-show forfeits the session. Exceptions at David's discretion.
- Between-session contact: Not available. Sessions are where the work happens.

**Acknowledgment:**
By signing below, both parties agree to the terms of this coaching relationship and commit to showing up fully for the work.

Client signature / Date
David Stone, Cornerstone Coaching Solutions LLC / Date

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the package, verify:

- Intake questionnaire is 7-10 questions, open-ended, no yes/no answers
- Welcome email is under 200 words and sounds like David, not a template
- What to Expect document fits on one page
- Coaching agreement clearly distinguishes coaching from therapy
- Gifted session language is present if applicable
- Client type calibrations are applied throughout
- No em dashes anywhere
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
- Correct entity name used (Cornerstone or Team Care)
