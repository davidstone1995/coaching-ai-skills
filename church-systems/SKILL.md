# Church Systems Consulting

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

Most churches are not struggling because their people are uncommitted or their pastor is untalented. They are struggling because they have no system. A great Sunday service can produce a visitor who never returns -- not because nobody cared, but because there was no follow-up system. A church can have a generous congregation and a chronic giving shortfall because there is no stewardship system. David has seen this from the inside, and he has seen what changes when systems are in place.

This skill produces materials for consulting and coaching church leaders on the systems that make a healthy church function: assimilation, follow-up, groups, stewardship, serving, and communication.

---

## The Framework

### Nelson Searcy / Church Leader Insights

The backbone. Nelson Searcy developed one of the most practical church systems frameworks in evangelical ministry, built around specific, reproducible systems that any church can implement regardless of size or budget. Key systems:

**The Assimilation System:** How a first-time guest becomes a fully connected member. The seven-step process (or variation thereof) from first visit to serving. The most critical system in any church -- the front door that either leaks or retains.

**The Follow-Up System:** What happens in the 24-48 hours after a first-time guest visits. Research shows the single biggest predictor of a guest returning is a personal contact from a church leader within 24 hours. Most churches have no system for this.

**The Groups System:** How a church connects people into community through small groups, Sunday school, or a similar structure. Includes a clear invitation pathway, group leader development, and launch rhythm.

**The Stewardship System:** How a church teaches generosity and builds a culture of giving. Includes first-time giving moments, generosity teaching series, year-end giving campaigns, and ongoing stewardship communication.

**The Serving System:** How a church moves attenders into ministry roles. The serving team as the primary assimilation pathway. Gift discovery and placement.

**The Communication System:** How a church communicates clearly, consistently, and strategically without overwhelming people. Hierarchy of communication channels, message discipline, and a content calendar.

### The Journey Church (Boca Raton, FL)

David served on staff at The Journey Church, one of the churches that pioneered the systems-based approach to church growth in the early 2000s. The Journey's model demonstrated that healthy systems produce healthy growth -- and that most church growth problems are really systems problems in disguise.

This is not theoretical for David. He has seen these systems work in a real church context. That experience is the credibility behind any consulting engagement.

## Consulting Categories

### Systems Audit

A diagnostic of one or more church systems. Produces a clear picture of what is working, what is broken, and what is missing -- with a prioritized set of recommendations.

### Implementation Planning

Helps a church leader build a plan to implement a specific system. Produces a step-by-step action plan with timeline, roles, and milestones.

### Consulting Session Prep

Prepares David for a consulting conversation with a church leader. Produces a session brief, key questions, and a recommended focus based on what David knows about the church.

### Workshop / Teaching

A teaching or workshop on church systems for a group of pastors, church staff, or ministry leaders. Produces an outline and facilitation guide.

For each system being audited, assess three things:

[Yes / No / Partial. Is there any defined process for this function, or is it informal and dependent on individuals remembering?]

**Is the system working?**
[Evidence-based assessment. What are the metrics? What do they show? A follow-up system that exists but produces no second visits is not working.]

**What is the highest-leverage improvement?**
[The one change that would produce the biggest result. Not a list of everything wrong. The single most important lever.]

---

**ASSIMILATION SYSTEM AUDIT**

Key questions:
- What is the first-time guest experience from parking lot to exit?
- What happens in the 24 hours after a first-time guest visits?
- How does a first-time guest become a regular attender?
- How does a regular attender become a member or committed participant?
- What percentage of first-time guests return for a second visit? (Benchmark: 20-30% is healthy)

What to look for:
- Is there a defined follow-up process, or does it depend on someone's memory?
- Is there a clear next step communicated to first-time guests (connection card, app, welcome class)?
- Is there a welcome class or starting point class, and does it lead somewhere?
- Who owns assimilation? If no one specific owns it, it is not a system -- it is a hope.

---

**FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM AUDIT**

Key questions:
- What happens when someone visits for the first time -- today, not in theory?
- Who contacts first-time guests? How? When?
- What is the follow-up sequence -- first contact, second contact, handoff to a group or ministry?
- How are contacts tracked? (Spreadsheet? CRM? Paper cards?)

What to look for:
- Personal contact within 24-48 hours (the most important variable)
- Multiple touch points over 2-4 weeks for non-responding guests
- Warm handoff to a next step (small group, serving opportunity, discipleship pathway)
- A CRM or tracking system that does not require a human to remember every name

*CV Pro Tools note:* If the church has no follow-up system, CV Pro Tools is a natural recommendation -- it automates the follow-up process. Present it as a resource, not a sales pitch. David uses it himself and can speak to it from experience.

---

**GROUPS SYSTEM AUDIT**

Key questions:
- How does a church attender get connected to a small group or community?
- What percentage of Sunday attenders are in a group? (Benchmark: 50-70% in healthy churches)
- How are group leaders identified, trained, and supported?
- What is the launch rhythm -- how often does the church on-ramp new groups?

---

**STEWARDSHIP SYSTEM AUDIT**

Key questions:
- Does the church teach generosity systematically or only during crises?
- Is there a first-time giving moment built into the guest journey?
- Does the church run an annual generosity series? When? How is it received?
- What is the year-end giving strategy?
- How does the church communicate with givers vs. non-givers differently?

---

**COMMUNICATION SYSTEM AUDIT**

Key questions:
- How many communication channels does the church use? (More than 3-4 is usually too many)
- Is there a clear hierarchy -- what goes where?
- Who controls the communication calendar? Is there a calendar?
- Does the congregation know what to expect and where to look?

## Consulting Session Prep

Same structure as coaching session prep (see session-prep skill) but focused on systems.

**Opening question:**
"Walk me through what happens when someone visits your church for the first time -- from the moment they pull into the parking lot to the moment they get home. Tell me exactly what happens."

This question reveals everything. The gaps between what the pastor describes and what actually happens are where the systems work lives.

**The systems behind the presenting problem:**
Most pastors come to a systems conversation with a surface problem ("we can't retain people," "giving is down," "we can't find volunteers"). The consulting session digs to the system underneath: "You can't retain people" is almost always an assimilation or follow-up problem. "Giving is down" is almost always a stewardship teaching or communication problem. Name the system behind the problem before building the solution.
