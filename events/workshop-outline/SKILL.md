# Workshop Outline

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

A workshop is different from a retreat. The group is usually larger, the time is shorter, and the participants may not know David yet. The job is to earn trust quickly, teach one thing well, and leave the room wanting more -- or better yet, already moving.

This skill builds the full workshop outline: opening, content arc, discussion questions, activities, and a close. It also produces a one-page facilitator brief David uses on the day.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Workshop topic or focus
- Audience type (church staff, ministry leaders, business owners, coaching cohort, mixed)
- Length (45 min, 60 min, 90 min, half-day)
- Format (keynote + discussion, breakout, interactive training, seminar)

**Helpful:**
- What the sponsor or organizer wants the group to walk away with
- What David knows about this specific audience
- Any prior content or context the workshop builds on
- Whether Q&A is expected and when
- Room setup (theater, round tables, small groups)

---

## Step 1: Identify the Workshop Type

**Short keynote + discussion (45-60 min):** David speaks 25-35 minutes, then opens for discussion or Q&A. Best for conferences, staff days, or association gatherings. Goal: one idea planted well, not five ideas planted quickly.

**Interactive training (60-90 min):** More participant engagement. Shorter content blocks, more discussion, some individual or small group activity. Best for teams, cohorts, or professional development days. Goal: a skill practiced or a conversation started that the participants own.

**Half-day seminar (3-4 hours):** Multiple content blocks with significant discussion time. Functions closer to a retreat but with a teaching spine. Best for a leadership cohort, church staff intensive, or Cornerstone workshop series.

## Step 2: Generate the Workshop Outline

Produce three components.

---

### COMPONENT 1: Workshop Outline (David's facilitation guide)

**[Workshop Title]**
[Audience] | [Length] | [Date / Event]
Cornerstone Coaching Solutions | cornerstonecoaching.solutions

---

**OPENING (5-10 min)**

*Goal:* Get people in the room. Earn the right to go somewhere with them.

[Opening approach. Not "tell them what you're going to tell them." Something that creates immediate connection or tension. Options:
- A story that opens the topic
- A question the room doesn't expect
- A statement that names what everyone is already thinking but hasn't said
- A brief reflection prompt that connects each person to the topic before David says anything]

*Opening question (if appropriate):* [A low-stakes opening question the whole room can answer. Sets up what is coming without giving it away.]

---

**CONTENT BLOCK 1 (10-15 min)**

*What David is teaching:* [The first idea. The foundational piece. What the rest builds on. Keep it clear and human -- not academic, not jargon-heavy. One idea. Said well. Then let it land before moving.]

*One story or example that makes this real:* [This is where David's 30 years matter. A story from ministry, coaching, chaplaincy, or his own life that makes the idea tangible. Not a case study. A real moment.]

*Transition:* [One sentence bridging to the next section. Don't summarize. Move forward.]

---

**DISCUSSION / ACTIVITY 1 (10-15 min)**

*Prompt or question:* [Something the group processes together -- in pairs, small groups, or as a full room. Calibrated to room size and setup. One question is better than three.]

*Debrief question:* [After the discussion, one question to bring it back: "What did you hear in your conversation that you want to bring back to the room?"]

---

**CONTENT BLOCK 2 (10-15 min)**

[Second content section. Builds on or deepens the first. Often the "so what" or the "here's how this actually works in real life."]

*Story or example:* [Different person, different situation, same principle.]

*Tension to name:* [Every good workshop has a moment where David names the real obstacle. Not the theoretical one -- the one people in the room are sitting with. This is the moment the room leans in.]

---

**DISCUSSION / ACTIVITY 2 (10-15 min)**

[A slightly deeper discussion than the first. Individual reflection or a more challenging pair/group prompt. This is where something real can surface if the group is willing.]

*Individual prompt (written, 3-5 min):* [A question each person answers for themselves before the group talks. Silence first. Then conversation.]

---

**CLOSING (10-15 min)**

*The one thing:* [The single thing David wants every person to leave with. Not a summary of everything he said. One sentence. Said simply. Repeated twice.]

*The invitation:* [What David is asking them to do with what they heard. Not an assignment. An invitation. Specific enough to act on, open enough to own. Example: "Before this week is over, have one conversation you have been putting off." Or: "Write down the one thing you are going to do differently starting today."]

*Close:* [How David ends. Brief. Human. Not a pep talk. Not a formula. One honest sentence that sends them out.]

---

**Q&A (if included)**

[If Q&A is planned, note where it falls in the outline and approximately how long. David's approach: answer the question asked, not the question he wishes they had asked. If a question is too complex for the room, offer to talk afterward.]

---

### COMPONENT 2: Slide or Handout Notes (optional)

If David is using slides or a handout, note the key content points that belong on a visual. Keep slides to single ideas or questions. Nothing on a slide that David is going to read out loud.

Key points from this outline:
- [List 3-5 single-sentence points that could appear as slide text or handout headers]

---

### COMPONENT 3: Facilitator Prep Note

One page. Morning-of read.

**[Workshop Title] -- Facilitator Prep Note**

**What this audience needs:** [One honest sentence. Not what the sponsor said they need -- what they actually need based on what David knows about them.]

**Where it might stall:** [The moment in the outline where energy tends to drop or resistance might appear. Usually the second content block, or the first time David asks something real. Know it's coming. Don't panic. Let it breathe.]

**The one line that has to land:** [The single sentence from the outline that is the heart of the whole thing. If nothing else gets through, this has to.]

**Time check points:** [For [length] format: [specific time] = should be finishing Content Block 1. [Specific time] = should be in Discussion 2. If behind, [what to trim]. If ahead, [where to add time].]

---

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the outline:

- Opening earns the room before asking anything of it
- Content blocks teach one idea at a time, not several
- Discussion questions move from accessible to honest
- The "one thing" is genuinely singular -- not a list
- Facilitator prep note is specific to this audience
- Format matches the workshop length
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
- Entity is Cornerstone Coaching Solutions
