# Retreat Planning

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

A retreat is a concentrated version of what coaching does in individual sessions: create the conditions for real conversation, honest self-reflection, and movement forward. The difference is that you have more time, you are away from normal patterns, and the group dynamic itself becomes part of the work.

This skill builds the full retreat package: agenda, session outlines, discussion frameworks, facilitator notes, and any pre-retreat communication needed.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Retreat format: half-day, full day, or Fri/Sat intensive
- Group type (church staff, ministry leaders, coaching cohort, small business team, athletes/Team Care, premarital cohort)
- Number of participants (approximate)
- Primary focus or theme (if one has been determined)

**Helpful:**
- Any specific outcomes the group or sponsor wants
- What David already knows about this group -- their dynamics, their tensions, what they need
- Whether this is a standalone event or part of a series
- Any prior content (assessment results, workshop, prior sessions) the retreat should build on
- Venue type (on-site, off-site, indoor, outdoor)

---

## Step 1: Identify the Format and Group

### Half-Day Intensive (3-4 hours)

One focused theme. Two to three sessions with breaks. Best for a team that needs one specific conversation or a group that cannot commit to a full day.

**Structure:**
- Opening: 20-30 min
- Session 1: 60-75 min
- Break: 15 min
- Session 2: 60-75 min
- Closing: 20-30 min

### Full Day

Broader scope. Room for more depth. Can handle multiple interconnected themes or one theme explored at three levels (individual, relational, organizational).

**Structure:**
- Morning: Opening + Session 1 + Session 2
- Lunch: Built-in relationship time (not wasted)
- Afternoon: Session 3 + Action planning + Close

### Fri/Sat Intensive ($1,500/mo pricing tier)

Two days. Used for coaching cohorts, leadership team intensives, or immersive experiences with a group that is serious about change.

**Structure:**
- Friday: Arrive, orientation, Session 1, dinner (relational)
- Saturday: Session 2, Session 3, Action planning, Debrief and close

## Step 2: Generate the Retreat Package

Produce four components. The agenda goes to the sponsor/group. Session outlines and the facilitator note stay with David.

---

### COMPONENT 1: Agenda (participant-facing)

One page. Clean. Enough structure to feel organized, not so much detail that it kills the spontaneity.

**[Retreat Name]**
[Group Name] | [Date(s)] | [Location]
Hosted by Cornerstone Coaching Solutions | cornerstonecoaching.solutions

---

[Half-day example structure:]

**[Time] -- Welcome and Opening**
[One sentence description of what this time is for. Keep it inviting, not clinical.]

**[Time] -- Session 1: [Session Title]**
[One sentence. What will happen, not everything that will be covered.]

**[Time] -- Break**

**[Time] -- Session 2: [Session Title]**
[One sentence.]

**[Time] -- Closing and Next Steps**
[One sentence.]

---

### COMPONENT 2: Session Outlines (David's facilitation guide)

For each session, produce a full outline with:

**Session [#]: [Title]**

*Goal for this session:* [What participants should leave with -- not learn, leave with. A decision, a realization, a commitment, an honest conversation they had not had before.]

*Opening question or activity (5-10 min):*
[Something that gets people into the room and into the topic. Not a team-building game. A real question or a brief reflection that connects to the session focus.]

*Content or framework to present (10-20 min):*
[If David is teaching anything, it lives here. Keep it brief. Teaching is the frame -- not the main event. The conversation is the main event.]

*Discussion or coaching questions (20-40 min):*
[4-6 questions. In sequence -- from surface to depth. The first question any room can answer. The last question only a group with courage will get to. Leave the last question open; don't answer it for them.]

*Individual reflection prompt (5-10 min):*
[One written prompt. People need a moment to think before they speak. Silence is not wasted time. Example: "Take 5 minutes. Write down: what is the one thing you came into today needing to say but haven't said yet?"]

*Closing the session:*
[How David brings it home. A bridge to the next session. A moment of naming what happened. No forced enthusiasm. One honest observation and a clear transition.]

---

### COMPONENT 3: Discussion Frameworks

Provide these as tools David can pull from during organic discussion:

**The Coaching Sandwich adapted for groups:**
- Top Bread: "What does this group most need to talk about today?"
- Lettuce: "What's the current reality around this topic for you?"
- Tomato: "What needs to happen today for this to feel like time well spent?"
- Protein: [Awareness questions for the group]
- Action: "What is one thing this group is going to do differently after today?"
- Bottom Bread: "What are you taking with you?"

**When the room goes quiet:**
- "What are you thinking but not saying?"
- "What is the group avoiding right now?"
- "Who has been wanting to say something for the last five minutes?"

**When one person dominates:**
- "I want to make sure we're hearing from everyone. [Name], what do you think?"
- "Let's go around. One sentence from each person."

**When things get honest:**
- Don't rescue. Don't fix. Let it land.
- "Thank you for saying that. Who else is sitting with something similar?"

---

### COMPONENT 4: Facilitator Prep Note

One page. David reads this the morning of.

**[Retreat Name] -- Facilitator Prep Note**
[Date]

**What this group needs most:** [One honest sentence about what this specific group is walking in carrying. What is the thing underneath the topic? What is the conversation they are afraid to have? What will make today matter?]

**Watch for:**
- [Dynamic or pattern to notice. Example: "This team has a conflict they have been managing around for six months. If it surfaces, don't redirect -- that may be the most important thing that happens today."]
- [Energy management note. Example: "They are coming off a hard stretch. The room may feel heavy. Start slower than planned if needed."]

**Pace guidance:** [Half-day: protect the depth moments, don't rush to cover everything. Full day: the afternoon is where real things happen -- save energy for it. Fri/Sat: Friday night sets the relational tone for Saturday. Don't skip it.]

**One thing to release:** [Something David might be tempted to control that he should let go of. Example: "You do not have to resolve everything today. Getting them talking honestly is enough."]

---

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the package:

- Agenda is participant-facing: clean, inviting, not clinical
- Session outlines follow the arc from surface to depth
- Discussion questions progress in sequence (any room can answer the first; only a courageous room reaches the last)
- Facilitator prep note is specific to this group and this day
- Format is appropriate to the retreat length
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
- Entity is Cornerstone Coaching Solutions (unless Team Care is specified)
