# Care Log

**Requires:** coaching-foundation

---

## What This Skill Does

Chaplaincy care is relational. The relationships are built visit by visit, conversation by conversation. The care log is how David remembers what matters -- what was said, who was struggling, who is worth checking in with next time.

This skill turns a voice note, a few bullet points, or a recalled summary into a clean, organized care log entry. Everything stays at the level of general observation -- no verbatim quotes, no clinical notes, nothing that would feel like a violation of the trust the relationship is built on.

---

## What You Need to Provide

**Required:**
- Visit context (MSU athletics, corporate client, or other Team Care context)
- Date
- Any people David connected with and what was discussed (provide as much or as little as you have)

**Helpful:**
- Specific situations or circumstances that came up
- Anyone who seemed to need follow-up
- Anything David wants to remember for next time
- Any referrals made or suggested

If David only has three bullet points from the visit, that is enough. The skill formats and expands what is there without adding what is not.

---

## Care Log Entry Format

---

**CARE LOG ENTRY**
[Context: MSU Athletics / Corporate Client Name] | [Date] | Team Care

---

**Who was contacted**

[First names or roles only. No full names in a log that might be shared or stored in a system. Example: "Head coach, two offensive linemen, trainer, equipment staff."]

**General summary of the visit**

[2-3 sentences. What was the tone of the room? What was the general atmosphere? Nothing specific enough to violate anyone's confidence -- just enough to remember what kind of visit it was. Example: "Good energy overall. Post-game week, lighter mood. Had a longer conversation with one player who has been on the periphery of the group."]

**Conversations of note**

[For any meaningful individual conversation, log it at the level of theme only -- not content. Example:
- "One player dealing with family pressure from home. Listening presence. No advice given. Will check in next visit."
- "Staff member mentioned burnout -- laughed it off. Worth watching."
- "One conversation that started casual and went somewhere real. Relationship deepening."

Do not record specifics that were shared in confidence. If someone shared something significant, the log records that the conversation happened and that follow-up is warranted -- not what was said.]

**Follow-up needed**

[Specific names or situations that need attention at the next visit. Keep this actionable. Example:
- "[Name] -- check in on the family situation. Don't bring it up unless they do."
- "[Name] -- follow up on the job thing they mentioned."
- "Text [name] before next visit -- said they'd be missing a few weeks."]

**Referrals made or suggested**

[If David pointed anyone toward a resource, a counselor, a pastor, or another professional -- note it here. Not the details of why -- just that the referral was made. Example: "Suggested [name] consider talking to someone outside the team context. Gave them nothing specific yet -- going to think about what fits."]

**For next visit**

[2-3 things to carry forward. Names to find, situations to notice, follow-up to complete. This becomes the seed of the next visit prep note. Example:
- "Find [name] -- they seemed to disappear early."
- "Room feels like it's in a good place -- keep showing up, don't manufacture depth."
- "Ask [name] how the conversation with [person] went."]

**Notable quote or moment (optional)**

[If something was said that David wants to remember -- not for confidentiality reasons but because it captured something true about the person or the room -- he can include it here. This is for David's reflection, not for reporting. Example: "He said something like, 'I didn't know chaplains were real people.' That one's worth holding."]

---

## Output Quality Check

Before delivering the log:

- No full names used (first names or roles only)
- Nothing recorded that would feel like a violation of confidence if accidentally seen
- Follow-up items are specific and actionable
- "For next visit" section has 2-3 real carry-forwards
- Entry is brief -- this is not a report, it is a memory aid
- No em dashes
- No banned phrases from coaching-foundation
- Entity is Team Care (not Cornerstone)
